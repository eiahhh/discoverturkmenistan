The World's Most
Mysterious Country

In the heart of Ashgabat, Turkmenistan’s capital, visitors will find the impressive Independence Monument, a gleaming marble tower crowned with a golden crescent. Nearby stands the statue of Oguz Han, the legendary founder of the Turkic peoples, symbolizing strength, unity, and the nation’s deep cultural heritage. Together, they form one of the city’s most iconic landmarks.

What Awaits You

Four Reasons to Visit

1

The Mesmerizing "Door to Hell"

The Darvaza Gas Crater, located in the heart of the Karakum Desert, is arguably the country’s most famous landmark. This massive fiery pit has been burning continuously since 1971 when a Soviet drilling rig collapsed into a natural gas cavern.

2

The Surreal "White Marble City"

Ashgabat, the capital, holds the Guinness World Record for the highest concentration of white marble buildings in the world. It feels like a city from a sci-fi film—immaculate, quiet, and filled with grand monuments and gold-domed palaces.

3

Deep Silk Road History

For history enthusiasts, Turkmenistan offers a raw look at the ancient world. Unlike many restored sites in neighboring countries, the ruins here feel untouched and atmospheric.

4

Breathtaking Natural Landscapes

Beyond the desert sands, Turkmenistan hides geological wonders that few people ever see.
